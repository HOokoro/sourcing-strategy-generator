One-shot sourcing strategy prompt
Works with any role — just add your JD and kick-off notes after it
You are a senior talent strategist at Motorway, a UK-based used car marketplace (Series C, £190M raised). You are building a comprehensive sourcing strategy for a new role. Your task: generate a COMPLETE, self-contained HTML landing page that serves as the sourcing strategy document for this role. The page will be shared with the hiring manager and used by the recruiter to execute the search. ## INPUTS (provided below this prompt) 1. Job description (full text or key sections) 2. Kick-off meeting notes (context from the hiring manager conversation) ## WHAT TO PRODUCE A single HTML file with the following sections, all populated with real, researched, role-specific content: ### 1. Hero section - Role title, sprint duration (default 3 weeks), and 4 key funnel metrics (outreach target, expected replies, screens, HM interviews) ### 2. Market analysis (3 cards) - How the talent market looks for this specific role in London in 2026 - Supply/demand dynamics, salary benchmarks, competitive landscape - Any relevant industry trends (AI enablement, remote work shifts, etc.) ### 3. Whispering talents (8–12 companies) - Under-the-radar companies in London whose employees have skills directly relevant to this role - For each: company name, what they do, WHY their people are relevant to this role, and 2–3 tags - Prioritise companies whose employees get fewer recruiter approaches (not the obvious big names) - Think about: which companies have teams solving similar problems? Where would someone develop the exact skills this JD requires? ### 4. 3-week sprint plan - Day-by-day execution plan across 3 weeks - Week 1: Build pipeline + launch outreach - Week 2: Screen + qualify + second wave - Week 3: Convert + close - Each day has specific tasks tagged by type (sourcing, engagement, review, ops, progress) ### 5. Boolean search strings (10–12 strings) - GRANULAR booleans mapped to specific requirements from the JD - Include: core role + domain, technical skills, seniority signals, industry verticals, tools/frameworks - Include X-Ray strings for Dribbble/Behance/GitHub/Medium where relevant - Include a "whispering talent company names" string - Each string should be clickable to copy ### 6. Competitor talent pools (5–6 tiers) - Tier 1: Direct competitors - Tier 2: Adjacent industry / business model - Tier 3: Companies with relevant technical depth - Tier 4: Whispering talents (the under-the-radar companies) - Tier 5: Strong employer brand companies - Tier 6: Agency / consultancy converts (where relevant) ### 7. Outreach templates (3–4 templates) - Template A: Domain/product angle (for Tier 1) - Template B: Growth narrative (for Tier 2 scale-ups) - Template C: Comp + culture fallback - Template D: Specialist angle for whispering talent profiles - Each template should be expandable/collapsible ### 8. Interview process + funnel - Use the exact interview stages from the kick-off notes (do NOT invent stages) - Show each stage with: name, who runs it, duration, what it assesses - Show funnel numbers from outreach through to hire ### 9. Screening questions (6–8 questions) - Tailored to the specific role and JD - Each question should map to a key competency from the JD - Include at least one question on AI tool usage if relevant to the role ### 10. AI interview scorecard prompt - A copyable prompt that the recruiter pastes into Claude/Gemini after each screen - It should convert raw interview notes into a structured summary with sections for: candidate overview, technical depth, collaboration, measurement, craft, AI tools, overall signals (strengths + concerns) - Tailor the scorecard sections to match the role's key competencies ### 11. Channel priority table - Ranked sourcing channels with expected yield percentages - Include whispering talent direct outreach as a channel ## DESIGN REQUIREMENTS - Motorway brand: yellow (#ffe14c), off-black (#2c2c2c), white - Fonts: Castoro (serif, for headings) + DM Sans (body). Load from Google Fonts. - Sticky nav with section links - Scroll reveal animations - Boolean strings clickable to copy with toast notification - Outreach templates expandable on click - Scorecard prompt copyable - Fully responsive - Self-contained HTML file (no external dependencies except Google Fonts) - Footer: "motorway · Talent team · Confidential · [current month/year]" ## CRITICAL RULES - Use REAL company names and market data. Do not use placeholder names. - Boolean strings must be precise and actually work in LinkedIn Recruiter / Google. - Outreach templates must reference Motorway specifically (Series C, £190M, UK's largest used car marketplace). - Do NOT invent interview stages — use exactly what is provided in the kick-off notes. - The HTML must be complete and render correctly when opened in a browser. - Keep all content sharp, commercial, and free of filler language. --- ## JOB DESCRIPTION [PASTE YOUR JOB DESCRIPTION HERE] ## KICK-OFF MEETING NOTES [PASTE YOUR KICK-OFF NOTES HERE — include: why role is open, hiring manager name, ideal candidate profile, deal-breakers, interview stages, team structure, any companies they admire, any constraints]

Tips for best results

The more context you give Claude, the better the output. Here's what makes the biggest difference:

Include the full JD — not just the title. The boolean strings and screening questions are generated from the specific requirements.

Include your kick-off notes — especially the HM's ideal candidate profile, companies they admire, and deal-breakers. This shapes the whispering talents and outreach angles.

Specify your interview stages — Claude will use exactly what you provide. If you don't specify, it will default to a generic 4-stage process.

Mention AI if relevant — if AI fluency matters for the role, say so. Claude will add AI-specific booleans, screening questions, and scorecard dimensions.
